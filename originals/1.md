# How AI can bring Einstein and Caesar to your classroom - some random thoughts about AI in Education

Imagine learning about the theory of relativity from Einstein himself. You could ask him any questions you have, watch him derive his famous equations on a blackboard, and see how he came up with his groundbreaking ideas.

Or imagine visiting a museum and exploring the Roman exhibit with a virtual guide. You could interact with historical figures like Julius Caesar, or chat with ordinary soldiers and farmers about their daily lives.

These scenarios might sound like science fiction, but they could become reality with the help of AI. AI has the potential to create immersive and engaging learning experiences that can boost your memory and understanding of any topic. Why? Because the more multi-dimensional and stimulating an event is, the more likely you are to remember it.

Think about it: traumatic events are hard to forget because they trigger strong emotions and associations in your brain. But so are positive events that involve active participation and discussion, or topics that you are passionate about. Immersiveness, in other words, is the key to effective learning. It's why many people say that the best way to learn a new language is to live in a country where it's spoken. You don't just memorize words and grammar rules; you learn how to use them in different contexts and situations, how to express yourself and connect with others. AI can add depth, context, emotion, and perspective to any subject matter, making it more interesting and memorable. It can create realistic simulations and scenarios that challenge your curiosity and creativity. It can personalize your learning journey and adapt to your needs and preferences.

Of course, AI is not perfect. As an adult, you might notice some flaws or inconsistencies in how it portrays certain characters or events. You might say that Einstein doesn't sound like himself, or that Caesar's accent is off. But for a child or a young adult, who faces critical learning milestones that can shape their future, these details are less important than the overall experience.

META may have given up on the metaverse, but that doesn't mean that we should abandon the idea of using virtual worlds for education. A metaverse lite could be a great platform for delivering these kinds of high-context and varied educational experiences.

And from a geopolitical perspective, it will be interesting to see how different countries adopt or reject AI in education, and what impact that will have on their long-term outcomes.
